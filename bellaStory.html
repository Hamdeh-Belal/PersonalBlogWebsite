Lost in a Daydream

Story

Bella sits by the window, her soft, graceful form silhouetted against the morning light. She gazes out into the distance, her eyes half-closed as if she’s lost somewhere between reality and a dream. What does she see? Maybe it’s the flutter of a bird’s wings, or the gentle sway of leaves in the breeze. But in Bella’s imagination, it’s more than that. Perhaps she envisions herself in a field of wildflowers, bathed in golden sunlight, where butterflies drift lazily and she moves as gracefully as a cloud.

She ponders the mysteries of the world beyond the glass with the serene wisdom of a philosopher. Every now and then, her gaze softens even further, and she sighs softly, as if something in the distance speaks to her heart. For Bella, this window is more than just a piece of glass—it’s a portal to all the quiet, beautiful places she dreams of.

As the minutes pass, Bella remains utterly still, as if afraid to break the spell of her daydream. In this moment, she’s not just a cat; she’s a poet, a dreamer, the queen of her own quiet universe, savoring the beauty of simply being.
